- Community page: https://www.johannite.org/community/

**Ecclesia Pistis Sophia / Sophian Fellowship**
- URL: https://www.sophian.org/ | https://www.ecclesiapistissophia.com/

## 2. INDIVIDUAL SCHOLARS

### Tier 1: Direct Gnostic Cosmology Specialists (Most Relevant)

**April D. DeConick**
- Position: Isla Carroll and Percy E. Turner Professor of New Testament and Early Christianity, Rice University
- URL: https://www.aprildeconick.com/ | https://profiles.rice.edu/faculty/april-deconick
- Academia.edu: https://rice.academia.edu/AprilDeConick
- Contact: Rice University, Religious Studies Department - MS 15, P.O. Box 1892, Houston, TX 77251
- Co-founder and Executive Editor, *Gnosis: Journal of Gnostic Studies* (Brill). Founded and chaired the Mysticism, Esotericism and Gnosticism group at SBL, and chaired the Nag Hammadi and Gnosticism group.
- Why relevant: THE gatekeeper for academic publication on Gnosticism. If your structural mapping is rigorous, submitting to her journal or reaching out directly is the highest-leverage move. Her Rice program (GEM) is the largest doctoral concentration in Gnosticism, Esotericism, and Mysticism in the world.

**Dylan M. Burns**
- Position: Senior Assistant Professor, History of Esotericism in Late Antiquity, University of Amsterdam
- URL: https://www.uva.nl/en/profile/b/u/d.m.burns/d.m.burns.html
- Academia.edu: https://uva.academia.edu/DylanBurns
- Research page: https://www.amsterdamhermetica.nl/research/individual-research-projects/research-dr-dylan-burns/
- Contact: Universiteit van Amsterdam, Centre for the History of Hermetic Philosophy, Postbus 1622, 1000 BP Amsterdam, Netherlands. Visiting: Kloveniersburgwal 48, Bushuis, 1012 CX Amsterdam.
- Editor of *Nag Hammadi and Manichaean Studies* (2018-2030) and *Gnosis: Journal of Gnostic Studies* (2015-2030). PhD from Yale (Ancient Christianity).
- Why relevant: Works specifically on Sethian Gnosticism and its relationship to later Greek philosophy. His focus on the borderlines between traditions makes him receptive to cross-domain structural analysis. Has appeared on SHWEP discussing Sethian cosmology and the Pleroma.

**Lautaro Roig Lanzillotta**
- Position: Professor of New Testament and Early Christian Studies, University of Groningen
- URL: https://www.rug.nl/staff/f.l.roig.lanzillotta/
- Academia.edu: https://rug.academia.edu/LautaroRoigLanzillotta
- Research portal: https://research.rug.nl/en/persons/lautaro-roig-lanzillotta/
- Co-founder and Editor-in-Chief, *Gnosis: Journal of Gnostic Studies*. Editor-in-Chief, *Nag Hammadi Bibliography Online*.
- Why relevant: His research project "Religious and Philosophical Currents in Late Antiquity and their Resonances in Modern Thought" explicitly bridges ancient and modern. Has published on Gnosticism's reception in modern literature and film. Would likely be receptive to a formal/structural mapping if presented rigorously.

**Nicola Denzey Lewis**
- Position: Margo L. Goldsmith Chair in Women's Studies in Religion, Claremont Graduate University
- URL: https://www.cgu.edu/people/nicola-denzey-lewis/
- Academia.edu: https://claremont.academia.edu/NicolaDenzeyLewis
- Author of *Cosmology and Fate in Gnosticism and Graeco-Roman Antiquity: Under Pitiless Skies* (Brill, 2013). Trained at Princeton under Peter Brown (history) and Elaine Pagels (Gnosticism).
- Why relevant: Her book is literally about Gnostic cosmology. She examines the structural relationship between cosmological systems. The subtitle "Under Pitiless Skies" addresses how Gnostic cosmology framed the material cosmos as a system of constraint -- directly analogous to an information-theoretic reading.

**Kevin Corrigan**
- Position: Samuel Candler Dobbs Professor of Interdisciplinary Humanities, Emory University
- URL: https://mesas.emory.edu/people/biographies/Corrigan-Kevin.html
- ResearchGate: https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Kevin-Corrigan-2
- Co-edited *Plato's Parmenides and its Heritage, Vol. II: Reception in Patristic, Gnostic, and Christian Neoplatonic Texts* (Brill, 2010). Co-edited *Gnosticism, Platonism and the Late Ancient World* (Brill, 2013).
- Why relevant: Works at the exact intersection of Neoplatonic emanation theory and Gnostic systems. His interdisciplinary humanities position signals openness to cross-domain interpretation.

**John D. Turner** (deceased 2019, but his students and intellectual lineage remain active)
- Was Cotner Professor of Religious Studies, University of Nebraska
- Major work: *Sethian Gnosticism and the Platonic Tradition* (2001). Demonstrated that Sethian treatises have "profound implications for understanding the origins of Neoplatonism, including its theory of dynamic emanationism."
- Why relevant: His work explicitly addresses the formal structure of emanation and its relationship to Platonic metaphysics. His students (many now active scholars) would be natural contacts. The festschrift in his honor (*Gnosticism, Platonism and the Late Ancient World*, Brill 2013) contains the network.

### Tier 2: Broader Esotericism and Interdisciplinary Scholars

**Jeffrey J. Kripal**
- Position: J. Newton Rayzor Chair in Philosophy and Religious Thought, Rice University
- URL: https://profiles.rice.edu/faculty/jeffrey-j-kripal | https://kripal.rice.edu/
- Created the GEM Program (Gnosticism, Esotericism, and Mysticism) at Rice -- the largest of its kind in the world. Associate Director, Center for Theory and Research, Esalen Institute. Published "Reflections of an American Gnostic" in *Gnosis: Journal of Gnostic Studies* 5:1 (2020). Working on a three-volume study connecting paranormal currents, esoteric literature, and science fiction.
- Why relevant: Kripal is the scholar most likely to find a structural/information-theoretic mapping of Gnostic cosmology intellectually exciting. His work explicitly bridges esoteric traditions with modern frameworks (consciousness studies, science fiction, systems thinking). He is at Rice alongside DeConick.

**Wouter J. Hanegraaff**
- Position: Professor of History of Hermetic Philosophy and Related Currents, University of Amsterdam
- URL: https://www.uva.nl/en/profile/h/a/w.j.hanegraaff/w.j.hanegraaff.html | https://www.wouterjhanegraaff.net/
- Academia.edu: https://uva.academia.edu/WouterHanegraaff
- Editor of *Dictionary of Gnosis & Western Esotericism* (Brill, 2005). Former president, European Society for the Study of Western Esotericism. Member, Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences.
- Why relevant: The leading figure in establishing Western esotericism as a legitimate academic field. His focus on "rejected knowledge in Western culture" means he's attuned to how marginalized intellectual traditions contain structural insights the mainstream missed. At Amsterdam alongside Dylan Burns.

**Hugo Lundhaug**
- Position: Professor of Biblical Reception and Early Christian Literature, University of Oslo
- URL: https://uio.academia.edu/HugoLundhaug
- Project: NEWCONT (New Contexts for Old Texts), ERC-funded. Focus on Nag Hammadi codices in their 4th-5th century contexts.
- Contact: Faculty of Theology, University of Oslo (https://www.tf.uio.no/)
- Why relevant: His approach emphasizes re-contextualizing the Nag Hammadi texts -- reading them in new frameworks. Would be receptive to a structural reinterpretation.

**Jason BeDuhn**
- Position: Professor of Religious Studies, Northern Arizona University
- URL: https://nau.academia.edu/JasonBeDuhn | https://experts.nau.edu/en/persons/jason-beduhn/
- Former Guggenheim Fellow. Specialist in Manichaeism and ancient Christianities. On editorial board of *Gnosis* journal.
- Why relevant: Manichaeism is a Gnostic tradition with particularly explicit cosmological structure (light particles, archons as information-constraining systems). BeDuhn's ritual/embodiment focus could connect to an information-theoretic reading of gnosis as signal recovery.

## 3. PUBLICATIONS AND JOURNALS

### Academic Journals

**Gnosis: Journal of Gnostic Studies (Brill)**
- URL: https://brill.com/view/journals/gnos/gnos-overview.xml
- Facebook: https://www.facebook.com/gnosisjournal/
- Author instructions: https://brill.com/fileasset/downloads_products/Author_Instructions/GNOS.pdf
- Submission: https://www.editorialmanager.com/gnos/mainpage.html
- Executive Editors: April D. DeConick (Rice), Lautaro Roig Lanzillotta (Groningen)
- Editorial Board includes: Dylan M. Burns, Jason BeDuhn, Malcolm Choat, Kevin Corrigan, Nicola Denzey Lewis, Ismo Dunderberg, Claire Fanger, Paul Foster, Majella Franzmann, Andre Gagne, Iain Gardner
- Founded 2016. Peer-reviewed. Indexed in Scopus. Devoted to Gnostic religious currents from antiquity to present. Explicitly aims for "cross-cultural and trans-historical conversations."
- Why relevant: THE venue for academic Gnostic studies. A paper mapping Gnostic cosmological structure to information theory would fit their stated mission of trans-historical conversation. This is your primary publication target.

**Aries: Journal for the Study of Western Esotericism (Brill)**
- URL: https://brill.com/view/journals/arie/arie-overview.xml
- Published under the auspices of ESSWE. First academic journal devoted to the study of esotericism. Publishes in English, French, German, Italian. Double-blind peer review.
- Covers Gnosticism, Hermetism, theurgy, occult sciences, Kabbalah, Rosicrucianism, theosophy, etc.
- Why relevant: Broader scope than *Gnosis*. A paper connecting Gnostic emanation to information theory might fit better here if it draws on multiple esoteric traditions (e.g., Kabbalistic sephiroth as parallel emanation hierarchy).

**Nag Hammadi and Manichaean Studies (Brill, book series)**
- Edited by Dylan M. Burns (2018-2030). Monograph series for substantial works on Nag Hammadi texts.

## 4. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### Highest-Priority Contacts for a Structural/Information-Theoretic Mapping

1. **April DeConick** (Rice) -- Gatekeeper. Her journal *Gnosis* is the publication venue. Her GEM program is the academic home.
2. **Jeffrey Kripal** (Rice) -- Most likely to be intellectually excited by the mapping. At Rice alongside DeConick.
3. **Dylan Burns** (Amsterdam) -- Journal editor, Sethian specialist, focuses on cross-tradition structural analysis.
4. **Lautaro Roig Lanzillotta** (Groningen) -- Journal co-founder, explicitly researches resonances between ancient currents and modern thought.
5. **Nicola Denzey Lewis** (Claremont) -- Wrote the book on Gnostic cosmology. Her framing of the material cosmos as a constraint system is closest to an information-theoretic reading.
6. **Kevin Corrigan** (Emory) -- Neoplatonic emanation + Gnostic systems, interdisciplinary humanities.
7. **Cyd Ropp** (independent) -- Already working at the exact intersection. Fastest path to engagement and potential collaboration.

### Highest-Priority Communities

1. **SHWEP** -- Request to appear as a guest. The host engages with exactly this kind of structural analysis.
2. **Aeon Byte Gnostic Radio** -- Largest Gnostic audience. Miguel Conner is receptive to novel interpretations.
3. **Sophian.org Forum** -- Active, Sophia-centered, takes cosmological structure seriously.
4. **Gnostic Hub Discord** -- Largest online Gnostic community.

### Publication Strategy

1. Submit to *Gnosis: Journal of Gnostic Studies* (Brill) -- primary target.
2. Consider *Aries* (Brill) if the mapping spans multiple esoteric traditions.
3. Present at ESSWE conference or SBL annual meeting (Nag Hammadi section).
4. Post preprint to Academia.edu for early feedback from the scholar network.
